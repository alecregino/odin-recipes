How to make Chicken Adobo

Chicken Adobo is a popular national dish in the Philippines and one of my personal favourites. Have you ever noticed how long the descriptions on recipes are? This image is also huge. I should probably fix that.

Anyways, here's the ingredients for Chicken Adobo.

Ingredients

Chicken and Marinade

  • 1.5 lb Chicken legs, thighs, whatever you want
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce, light or ordinary all purpose
  • 1/3 cup + 2 tbsp of white vinegar
  • 4 bay leaves (fresh) or 3 dried

For Cooking

  • 2 tbsp oil, vegetable
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 small brown onion, diced
  • 1 1/12 cups (375ml) or water
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp whole black pepper

For Serving

  • 2 green onions/scallions

Instructions

  1. Combine Chicken and marinade ingredients in a bowl. Marinate for at least 20 minutes, or up to overnight.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a skillet over high heat. Remove chicken from marinade (reserve marinade) and place in the pan. Sear both sides until browned. Do not cook chicken all the way through
  3. Remove chicken skillet and set aside
  4. Heat the remaining oil in skillet. Add garlic and onion, cook for 1 1/2 minutes
  5. Add the reserved marinade, water, sugar, and black pepper. Bring it to a simmer then turn heat down to medium high. Simmer 5 minutes
  6. Add chicken. Simmer uncovered for 20 to 25 minutes (no need to stir), turning chicken at around 15 minutes, until the sauce reduces down to a thick jam-like
  7. Coat chicken in glaze then serve over rice!
